# Mac trackpad gestures

Source: switchtomac.app

Most are on by default. You can see or change them in System Settings, under Trackpad.

### Tap to click
- Gesture: Tap with one finger
- What it does: A light tap clicks, no pressing down. Switch it on in Trackpad settings if it is not already.

### Right-click
- Gesture: Tap with two fingers
- What it does: This is your right-click. It opens the same menu the right mouse button did.

### Scroll
- Gesture: Two fingers, up or down
- What it does: Scroll any page, list, or document.

### Zoom in or out
- Gesture: Pinch or spread two fingers
- What it does: Zoom into photos, maps, and PDFs.

### Swipe between pages
- Gesture: Two fingers, left or right
- What it does: Go back and forward, like the browser arrows.

### Switch desktops and apps
- Gesture: Three or four fingers, sideways
- What it does: Slide between full-screen apps and desktops.

### Mission Control
- Gesture: Swipe up, three or four fingers
- What it does: See every open window laid out at once.

### App Exposé
- Gesture: Swipe down, three or four fingers
- What it does: Spread out all the windows of the app you are in.

### Show desktop
- Gesture: Spread thumb and three fingers
- What it does: Clear everything out of the way to reach your desktop.

### Show all apps
- Gesture: Pinch thumb and three fingers
- What it does: Open the grid of all your installed apps.
