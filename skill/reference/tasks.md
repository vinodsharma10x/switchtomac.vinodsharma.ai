# Everyday Mac tasks (coming from Windows)

Source: switchtomac.app

Things that do not map to a single keystroke, with the Windows way next to the Mac way.

### Right-click without a right button
- Category: Windows & apps
- Windows: Right mouse button
- Mac: ⌃ click
- How to: Click with two fingers on the trackpad; that's on by default. Prefer a corner? Turn on a bottom-right click in System Settings ▸ Trackpad ▸ Secondary click. Holding Control and clicking always works too.

### Take a screenshot of part of the screen
- Category: Screenshots
- Windows: Win + Shift + S
- Mac: ⌘ ⇧ 4
- How to: Press it, drag the crosshair over the area, and release, and it saves to your Desktop. Add Control (⌃⌘⇧4) to copy it to the clipboard instead.

### Screenshot or record your screen
- Category: Screenshots
- Windows: Win + G (Game Bar)
- Mac: ⌘ ⇧ 5
- How to: ⌘⇧5 opens one small toolbar that does both. For a still image, pick capture the whole screen, a window, or a selection. For video, choose Record Entire Screen or Record Selected Portion, then click Record. Recordings stop from the icon in the menu bar.

### Find and open an app
- Category: System & search
- Windows: Start menu / Win
- Mac: ⌘ Space
- How to: Tap ⌘Space, start typing the app's name, and press Return. Spotlight is your Start menu, Run box, and file search rolled into one.

### Force quit a frozen app
- Category: Windows & apps
- Windows: Ctrl + Alt + Delete
- Mac: ⌘ ⌥ ⎋
- How to: Opens the Force Quit window: pick the stuck app and click Force Quit. For live CPU and memory stats (the “Task Manager”), open Activity Monitor via Spotlight.

### Juggle all your open windows
- Category: Windows & apps
- Windows: Win + Tab (Task View)
- Mac: ⌃ ↑
- How to: ⌃↑ spreads every window out so you can click the one you want; ⌃↓ shows just the current app's windows; ⌘Tab flips between apps. On the trackpad, swipe up with three or four fingers.

### Type an emoji or accented letter
- Category: System & search
- Windows: Win + .
- Mac: fn E
- How to: fn E (or Control-Command-Space) opens the emoji picker. For accents, hold down a letter for a popup, or press fn twice for the full Character Viewer.

### Peek inside a file instantly
- Category: Files & Finder
- Windows: -
- Mac: Space
- How to: Click a file once in Finder and tap the Space bar to preview it full-size, no app needed. Tap Space again to close. There's no Windows equivalent; this one's a gift.

### Move a file instead of copying it
- Category: Files & Finder
- Windows: Ctrl + X then Ctrl + V
- Mac: ⌘ C then ⌥ ⌘ V
- How to: Copy the file with ⌘C, go to the destination, then press Option-Command-V to move it there. That's the Mac's version of cut-and-paste for files.

### Snap windows side by side
- Category: Windows & apps
- Windows: Win + Arrow
- Mac: drag to a screen edge
- How to: Drag a window against the left or right edge to fill half the screen, or into a corner for a quarter. The Window ▸ Move & Resize menu has more layouts. (Proper tiling arrived in macOS 15.)

### The Delete key only deletes backward
- Category: Text & cursor
- Windows: The Delete key
- Mac: fn ⌫
- How to: On a Mac, the key labelled Delete behaves like the Windows Backspace. To erase the character ahead of the cursor, press fn+Delete (or Control+D).

### Lock the screen or log out
- Category: System & search
- Windows: Win + L
- Mac: ⌃ ⌘ Q
- How to: ⌃⌘Q locks your Mac right away. To sign out completely, press ⇧⌘Q.

### Change a setting
- Category: System & search
- Windows: Win + I / Settings
- Mac: Apple menu ▸ System Settings
- How to: Click the Apple logo at the very top-left, then System Settings, the Mac's version of the Control Panel. Search the sidebar to jump straight to what you need.

### Where did my download go?
- Category: Files & Finder
- Windows: Downloads folder
- Mac: Dock & Finder sidebar
- How to: Downloads land in the Downloads folder, on the right side of the Dock and in every Finder window's sidebar. Screenshots save to the Desktop by default; change that under Options in the ⌘⇧5 toolbar.
